About the Project v1.0 · Nigeria

Bringing data clarity
to Nigeria's food markets

FoodPriceNG is an open-source machine-learning application that estimates the retail price of everyday food items across Nigerian markets — from government price surveys to supermarket chains and on-demand delivery platforms. We built this to put real market intelligence in the hands of ordinary Nigerians, free of charge.

The Problem We Are Solving
⚠ The Challenge

Nigerian food prices are volatile, opaque, and impossible to track

Nigeria's ongoing inflation crisis has hit food prices the hardest. Between 2022 and 2024, food inflation surged past 35%, with everyday staples like rice, cooking oil, and beans nearly doubling in cost within months. Yet ordinary households have no reliable tool to know what a fair price looks like before they shop.

Market data exists — scattered across government surveys, delivery apps, and supermarket records — but it is fragmented, inconsistent in format, and never synthesised into a single usable signal. Traders exploit this information gap. Consumers overpay. Policymakers react too slowly.

FoodPriceNG closes that gap. We collect data from 9 stores across multiple vendor types, apply a rigorous feature engineering pipeline, and deliver a machine-learning price estimate in seconds — free, for anyone with a phone or browser.

Core Capabilities
🤖

ML-Powered Predictions

A gradient-boosted model trained on historical Nigerian market data. Log-transformation of the target variable ensures strong accuracy across the full price range — from cheap staples to premium imports.

📅

Seasonal Intelligence

Date-derived features — month, quarter, and day — capture the seasonal price swings driven by Nigeria's agricultural cycles, festive demand surges, and currency fluctuation patterns.

📍

Geospatial Awareness

GPS coordinates allow the model to reflect price variation across neighbourhoods and store types — from an NBS government survey point to a Glovo delivery hub in Victoria Island.
